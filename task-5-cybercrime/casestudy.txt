Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

UPI fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India, especially among students and New users of digital payment platforms.
In This type of fraud, attackers trick victims into sending money or revealing sensitive details such as OTPs or UPI PINs.
A typical scenario begins when the victim receives a call or message from someone pretending to be a bank official or customer support agent. 
The fraudster may claim that there is an issue with the victim’s account or offer a reward or cashback. 
They then send a payment request or ask the victim to click on a fake link.
Once the victim enters their UPI PIN or approves the request, money is instantly deducted from their account.
Students and elderly people are often targeted due to lack of awareness. 
The consequences include financial loss, stress, and sometimes leakage of personal data.
This type of fraud highlights the importance of digital awareness and careful handling of online transactions.
